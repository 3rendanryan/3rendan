  2. What is this HELIN thing?

    3. HELIN Consortium

    The HELIN Consortium is a grouping of academic libraries in the Southeastern New England area that participate in joint ventures. PC patrons can borrow materials from these scholarly libraries if desired.

    Participating institutions include:

    • The University of Rhode Island
    • Wheaton College
    • Roger Williams University
    • Johnson & Wales University
    • Rhode Island College
    • The Community College of Rhode Island
    • Bryant University
  3. What institutions can I get materials from as a member of the PC community?

    4. Providence College undergraduates, continuing education students, graduate students, staff, clergy, and faculty can all borrow books and use the Brown University and HELIN systems for inter-library loans.

    HELIN Institutions include:

    • The University of Rhode Island
    • Wheaton College
    • Roger Williams University
    • Johnson & Wales University
    • Rhode Island College
    • The Community College of Rhode Island
    • Bryant University

     

    Persons with alumni cards, special cards, and retired PC faculty and staff can borrow books from the Phillips Memorial Library.

  7. My card will not work to access the databases / reserve materials through HELIN?

    8. Often patrons cannot access services remotely or reserve books online because their card has expired. All cards issued by Providence College expire in order to ensure the currency of record information.

     

    Please call the circulation desk for assistance. This matter can often be resolved over the phone.

  8. How can I get an alumni card and what privileges does it provide?
      9. Phillips Memorial Library recognizes all Providence College degree holders as Alumni. All Providence College Alumni may request a free library card by visiting the Circulation desk at Phillips Memorial Library during College Business hours (M-F, 8:30am-4:30pm).

      Alumni Benefits:

    • Book borrowing privileges at Phillips Memorial Library
    • On-site* access to electronic books and journals
    • Access to wireless network
    • Research assistance in person, by phone, email, IM or SMS (coming soon)

    Alumni Borrowing Periods:

    • Alumni may borrow up to 30 books at time for about 1 month
    • Alumni may request 1 renewal of books by telephone or by email.
